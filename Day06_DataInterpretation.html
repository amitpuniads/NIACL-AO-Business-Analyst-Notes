📊 Part 1: Data Interpretation Basics

Types of Data Representation

  1. Tables: Raw data in rows and columns
  2. Bar Charts: Vertical or horizontal bars for comparison
  3. Line Graphs: Shows trends over time
  4. Pie Charts: Shows percentage distribution
  5. Caselets: Data presented in paragraph form

🔑 Key Formulas for DI

1. Percentage:
Percentage = (Part / Whole) × 100

2. Percentage Change:
% Change = [(New Value - Old Value) / Old Value] × 100

3. Profit/Loss:
Profit = Selling Price - Cost Price
Profit % = (Profit / CP) × 100

4. Ratio:
If A:B = 3:5, and A = 15, then B = (15 × 5) / 3 = 25

5. Average:
Average = Sum of all values / Number of values

📈 Growth Rate & CAGR

Simple Growth Rate:
Growth Rate = [(Value_year2 - Value_year1) / Value_year1] × 100

CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate):
CAGR = [(Ending Value / Beginning Value)^(1/n) - 1] × 100
Where n = number of years
Example 1: Growth Rate
NIACL premium in 2022: ₹10,000 Cr
NIACL premium in 2023: ₹12,000 Cr
Growth Rate = [(12000 - 10000) / 10000] × 100 = 20%
Example 2: CAGR
Initial premium (2020): ₹8,000 Cr
Final premium (2024): ₹15,000 Cr
n = 4 years
CAGR = [(15000/8000)^(1/4) - 1] × 100
= [(1.875)^0.25 - 1] × 100
= [1.170 - 1] × 100 = 17.0%

Set 4: Caselet Questions (Q16-Q20)

Caselet: NIACL has 1000 employees. 40% work in Operations, 25% in Sales, 20% in Claims, and rest in Administration. Average salary in Operations is ₹8 lakh, Sales is ₹10 lakh, Claims is ₹9 lakh, and Administration is ₹7 lakh.

Set 5: Ratio & Proportion (Q21-Q25)

Q21. Divide ₹12,000 Cr premium in ratio 3:5:4 among Motor, Health, Life.
Solution: Total parts = 3 + 5 + 4 = 12
Motor = (3/12) × 12000 = ₹3000 Cr

Q22. If A:B = 2:3 and B:C = 4:5, find A:B:C
Solution: A:B = 2:3 = 8:12 (multiply by 4)
B:C = 4:5 = 12:15 (multiply by 3)
A:B:C = 8:12:15

Q23. Claims increase from ₹80 Cr to ₹100 Cr. Find percentage increase.
Solution: [(100-80)/80] × 100 = 25%

Q24. Price decreases by 20%. To restore, it should increase by?
Solution: Let original = 100, after decrease = 80
Required increase = [(100-80)/80] × 100 = 25%

Q25. A sum becomes ₹6000 from ₹5000 in 2 years. Find rate of interest.
Solution: Interest = 1000
SI = (P × R × T) / 100
1000 = (5000 × R × 2) / 100
R = 10%

Set 6: Speed, Distance, Time (Q26-Q30)

Q26. A surveyor travels 60 km at 30 km/h and returns at 20 km/h. Find average speed.
Solution: Average speed = (2 × 30 × 20) / (30 + 20) = 1200 / 50 = 24 km/h

Q27. A claim processor processes 120 claims in 3 hours. How many in 5 hours?
Solution: Rate = 120 / 3 = 40 claims/hour
In 5 hours = 40 × 5 = 200 claims

Q28. Distance = 150 km, Speed = 50 km/h. Time taken?
Solution: Time = Distance / Speed = 150 / 50 = 3 hours

Q29. Two agents 150 km apart travel towards each other at 40 km/h and 35 km/h. When meet?
Solution: Relative speed = 40 + 35 = 75 km/h
Time = 150/75 = 2 hours

Q30. Surveyor travels 120 km. First half at 40 km/h, second at 60 km/h. Average speed?
Solution: Time₁ = 60/40 = 1.5 hrs, Time₂ = 60/60 = 1 hr
Avg = 120/2.5 = 48 km/h

Set 7: Work & Time (Q31-Q35)

Q31. A can complete underwriting in 10 days, B in 15 days. Together in how many days?
Solution: A's 1 day work = 1/10
B's 1 day work = 1/15
Together = 1/10 + 1/15 = 5/30 = 1/6
Days = 6 days

Q32. 5 agents process 500 applications in 10 days. How many agents for 600 in 8 days?
Solution: M₁D₁W₂ = M₂D₂W₁
5 × 10 × 600 = M₂ × 8 × 500
M₂ = 7.5 ≈ 8 agents

Q33. A processes 60 claims/day, B processes 40 claims/day. Together for 500 claims?
Solution: Combined rate = 100 claims/day
Days = 500/100 = 5 days

Q34. 12 workers complete task in 8 days. How many workers for same task in 6 days?
Solution: M₁D₁ = M₂D₂
12 × 8 = M₂ × 6
M₂ = 16 workers

Q35. Team processes 1200 applications in 15 days, 8 hrs/day. Days for 2000 apps, 10 hrs/day?
Solution: (15 × 8)/1200 = (D₂ × 10)/2000
D₂ = 20 days

Set 11: Compound Interest (Q51-Q55)

Q51. ₹50,000 at 10% p.a. compounded annually. Amount after 3 years?
Solution: A = 50000(1.10)³ = ₹66,550

Q52. At what rate ₹8,000 becomes ₹10,000 in 2 years (CI)?
Solution: 1.25 = (1 + r/100)²
r = 11.8%

Q53. Principal ₹20,000, rate 12%, time 2 years. Find CI.
Solution: A = 20000(1.12)² = 25088
CI = 25088 - 20000 = ₹5,088

Q54. Difference between CI and SI for ₹10,000, 10%, 2 years?
Solution: SI = 2000
CI = 10000(1.1)² - 10000 = 2100
Difference = ₹100

Q55. In how many years ₹16,000 becomes ₹25,000 at 25% CI?
Solution: 1.5625 = (1.25)^n
n = 2 years

Set 13: Probability (Q61-Q65)

Q61. P(filing Motor claim) = 0.15, P(Health) = 0.20. P(at least one)?
Solution: P(A or B) = 0.15 + 0.20 - (0.15×0.20)
= 0.35 - 0.03 = 0.32 or 32%

Q62. In 100 policies, 5 fraudulent. P(all 3 selected are genuine)?
Solution: (95/100) × (94/99) × (93/98) = 0.856 or 85.6%

Q63. Two dice rolled. P(sum = 7)?
Solution: Favorable outcomes = 6 (1+6, 2+5, 3+4, 4+3, 5+2, 6+1)
Total = 36
P = 6/36 = 1/6

Q64. Bag has 5 red, 3 blue balls. P(both red when 2 drawn)?
Solution: (5/8) × (4/7) = 20/56 = 5/14

Q65. P(claim approved) = 0.85. Out of 10 claims, P(exactly 8 approved)?
Solution: Use binomial: C(10,8) × (0.85)⁸ × (0.15)²
= 0.276 or 27.6%

📐 Important Formulas Quick Reference

Percentage & Profit/Loss:
• % = (Part/Whole) × 100
• % Change = [(New-Old)/Old] × 100
• Profit% = (Profit/CP) × 100
• SP = CP × (100+Profit%)/100
• CP = SP × 100/(100+Profit%)

Interest:
• SI = (P×R×T)/100
• CI = P[(1+R/100)^T - 1]
• Difference (CI-SI for 2 years) = P(R/100)²

Time, Speed, Distance:
• Speed = Distance/Time
• Average Speed = Total Distance/Total Time
• Average Speed (same distance) = (2×S1×S2)/(S1+S2)
• Relative Speed (opposite) = S1 + S2
• Relative Speed (same direction) = |S1 - S2|

Work:
• Work = Time × Efficiency
• Combined work = 1/A + 1/B
• M1×D1×H1/W1 = M2×D2×H2/W2

Ratio & Proportion:
• If a/b = c/d then ad = bc
• If a:b = c:d, then (a+b):(a-b) = (c+d):(c-d)
• Compound ratio of a:b and c:d = ac:bd

Growth Rate:
• Simple Growth = [(End-Start)/Start] × 100
• CAGR = [(End/Start)^(1/n) - 1] × 100
• Doubling Time ≈ 72/Rate

Alligation (Mixture):
• (C-M):(M-D) where C=Costly, D=Cheaper, M=Mean
• Final quantity = (Q1×P1 + Q2×P2)/(Q1+Q2)
